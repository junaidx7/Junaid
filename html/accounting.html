Understanding Competitor
Pain Points

To comprehend the pain points of users and refine our approach, I delved into various sources.

Reddit : renowned as the holy grail of authentic user reviews, became a valuable platform. I scrolled through numerous user reviews, particularly those pertaining to competitor apps like QuickBooks and Xero. This allowed me to gain insights directly from users about their experiences and challenges with these applications.

Talking to accountants : I engaged in discussions with internal accountants within our company. Through these discussions, I sought to uncover specific pain points associated with QuickBooks. By directly consulting with professionals utilizing the software, I aimed to identify missing features and areas for improvement to enhance our own software's usability and effectiveness.

Competitor Analysis

I conducted a comprehensive competitor analysis, evaluating industry giants like Zoho Accounting and Xero. This analysis empowered me to understand their strengths and weaknesses, enabling me to strategically position our product. By learning from their approaches, I created a user-friendly and feature-rich design that sets Nathan Digital Accounting apart. This thorough analysis ensured our software not only competes but excels in the competitive landscape, offering users a superior accounting solution.

Creating
Sitemap

Information architecture is the method of organizing and labeling information to make it findable and understandable. when creating the information architecture for this accounting system, I prioritised the following aspects to ensure a user-friendly and efficient experience

  • Avoid unclear labels and categorisation: Labels and categories should be clear, consistent, and reflect the mental models of users.
  • Avoid Inconsistent navigation: Navigation paths should be logical and consistent across the system, avoiding dead ends and confusing transitions.

Usability Testing &
Iterations

i performed moderated usability testing for several core features of the software with 5 in house accountants in the first round of usability testing. i will be showcasing here how it was performed for the ‘create new invoice’ feature. Participants were provided with the software and assigned specific tasks to complete. The evaluation of usability was conducted in two ways.

Firstly, direct observation of the recording, of the users while performing the assigned tasks. Metrics such as time on task, error rate, and the number of clicks and several others were noted to gauge the efficiency and effectiveness of their interactions with the software. Additionally, users were asked few questions post-task.

These inquiries focused on assessing the perceived usefulness of specific features or functionalities, as well as identifying elements that users found easy or challenging. These two methods aimed to provide a comprehensive understanding of both quantitative performance metrics and user’s subjective experiences during the usability testing process.

Test Scenarios

Out of the many flows tested, just showcasing one here

Scenario 1 : User needs to generate a standard invoice for a client with basic details.

  • Navigate to the 'Create Invoice' option.
  • Add essential details like client name, invoice date, and due date.
  • Input line items with quantities and prices.
  • Save the invoice.

Scenario 2 : User needs to apply discounts and include tax in the invoice.

  • Add line items with discount percentages.
  • Include tax for specific items or the overall invoice.
  • Confirm that the final amount reflects discounts and tax accurately.

Scenario 3 :User wants to send the created invoice to a client via email.

  • After creating the invoice, locate the option to send via email.
  • Input the client's email address.
  • Confirm that the client receives the email with the attached invoice.
